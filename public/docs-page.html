Credentialing & Contracting

Credntailing & Contracting Worflow Explained:

Credentialing is a process of verifying a healthcare provider’s qualifications, training, and skills. It is also a process of checking whether a provider has the appropriate licenses or certifications that are required by law to practice the medical / healthcare services. Healthcare providers are required to be credentialed with a payer before they can enroll patients in that payer’s network. Credentialing is generally a manual process and it can take up to six weeks for the provider to be credentialed with a payer. In this documentation you can find the application process, credentialing guidance and contacts you may need.

Credentialing by definition?

Credentialing is the process of establishing the qualifications of licensed medical professionals and assessing their background and legitimacy. Credentialing is the process of granting a designation, such as a certificate or license, by assessing an individual's knowledge, skill, or performance level.

Contracting

Contracting Worflow Explained:

Along with the credentialing all most all the commercial carriares requires contracting to accept a provider to their network. Some of the payers process contracting application along with the credentialing but some of them complete credentialing upfront then they sent out contract for signature. This contract will include the reimbursment schedule, participating plan details, despute resolution etc.

Common Contracting process:
  1. Submission of initial application / request / paperworks.
  2. Contract Submission & credentialing.
  3. Deffeciancy Correction & Approval.
  4. System Loading & Welcome letter issuance.
Payer Networks / Plan Details

Network requirement defines which networks that provider / group can participate in, this will outlined in detail in the contract. During the contracting process it is a best practice to review the contract to list out the offered plan detail which basically the network (HMO, PPO etc.).

Fee-schedule / Reimbursment Schedule / Compensation Schedule

A fee-schedule is a complete listing of reimbursment offered by a health plan which will be mentioned in the contract with a sub-headline fee-schedule / reimbursment schedule. Usually when a payer does not have its own fee-scheule they follow Medicare or Medicad fee-schedule as standard and then will break it dow per percentage (Eg: Most of the MCOs follows MCD guildlines hence theire fee-schedule verbage would be something like so and so % of MCD.).


Always check out the reimbursment, plan details and get clarification from representative on any concerns may arise.
Reasons not to get a contract: Adequet provider avaialbility in the network, lack of experiance or licensure etc.
Medicare & most of Medicaids does not follow the contracting process they complete contracting and credentialing at the same time and it is commonly called enrollment. All the agreements will be included in the enrollment packet.

Onboarding a Physician

In this section we will check how to onboard a new physician to an existing clinic and how to onboard a solo practicing provider.
What are the information to collect from a credentialer standpoint to complete the credentialig process in an effective manner.


Information reqiured to onboard a physician to an existing group:
  1. Resume / CV
  2. CMS I & A Logins for Medicare Enrollment application
  3. CAQH Logins
  4. Medical License Copy
  5. Board / Specialty Certification
  6. DEA - If applicable
  7. Collaborating Physician Agreement - Incase of Midlevel Practitioner (NP, PA)
  8. Hospital Affliation / Admitting Priviliages (Required for PCP)
  9. Proffessional Liability Insurance (Either under the group / Individual)
  10. SSN, DOB, Home Address & Place of birthe (Country & City)
  11. Provider Role (PCP / Specialist)
  12. Adverse action / Malpractice report (If applicable)
With above listed information you can start the credentialing process with almost all the insurance. Alo its a best practice to verify the information using NPPES, USPS & Online license verification system to make sure all credentials are current & valid.

Onboarding Solo Practice

Solo practice is described by its name—a practice without partners or employment affiliations with other practice organizations. Solo practices are usually characterized by a small staff and typically have a limited patient base. This smaller size and the autonomy of being the only physician gives the advantage of being able to design, grow, and develop the practice as you'd like relative to other practice settings.


Information reqiured to onboard a Solo Practitioner:

Solo Practitioners are those who work independently at their home office or in a office setting without additional provider collaborations. Providers can utilize their SSN for enrollment and billing purpose when they do solo practising. The restriction would be if they want to add a new provider from a different specialy then at that poit he/she would need update / amend the existing contracts & enrollments.
In addition to the above list, you may need the below items to credential this type of provider.

  1. IRS Letter (CP575 /147C / W9 with SSN)
  2. Practice Address
Headsup: Submitting Medicare application for Solo Practitioner is slighly different, you may need to check the options available in the PECOS.
